In Defense of Jonathan Safran Foer


When being a modern philosopher is not enough

I read all his books, currently diving into "Here I Am" (got a signed copy, by the way) and the man is smart. "Eating Animals" left me feeling sick and vegetarian for half a year, "Extremely Loud and Incredibly Close" was one of the books I shared with one of the most interesting people to have crossed my life. Jonathan is far too personal for me to judge objeectively. I will do so. But when I have enough time. A lot more time.

While you wait for my exquisite insight into the matter, here you can listen to him read a beautiful story. Take your time. It's philosophy you're supposed to be researching now.
